## 1. Brand Snapshot

Tee Jaye's Country Place is a 56-year-old Central Ohio comfort food chain ("Down Home Cookin' at its Best since 1970") with 7 locations across the Columbus/Zanesville/Newark area. They serve all-day breakfast (their signature Barnyard Buster is a massive breakfast platter), homestyle lunch/dinner (country-fried steak, meatloaf, hot sandwiches, homemade soups), and seasonal specials (Lenten fish, Valentine's steak dinner). The brand identity is nostalgic, unpretentious, and family-oriented -- think Waffle House meets Cracker Barrel. Their Instagram is functional and promotional, not design-driven. Posts are primarily iPhone food photos and text-heavy promotional graphics with no consistent visual system. **Agent 3 should NOT attempt to replicate the current Instagram aesthetic -- instead, create a polished version of their warm, homestyle personality that elevates the brand while staying true to the "country place" identity.**

## 3. Visual System Blueprint

### Overall Aesthetic
**Primary:** UGC-heavy / Rustic-organic
**Secondary:** Corporate clean (for promotional graphics)

The feed has no deliberate visual system. Posts alternate between casual food photos and text-heavy promotional flyers with no consistent template, color scheme, or composition rules.

### Color Palette (from actual posts)
- **Dominant:** Warm browns and creams from breakfast food (eggs, biscuits, grits, gravy)

- **Feed color consistency:** Very inconsistent -- no planned grid or color theme

### Image Composition Patterns (static photos, last 3 months)
- **Camera angles:** ~80% overhead flat-lay (looking down at plates), ~20% slight angle
- **Close-up/wide ratio:** 100% close-up plate shots -- no wide/environment shots visible
- **Subject positioning:** Centered plate filling most of the frame
- **Edge-cropping:** None -- food is always fully contained within frame
- **Negative space:** Minimal -- plates fill the frame
- **People included:** Never in static photos
- **Surface/table details:** Tan/beige laminate diner table (typical for family restaurant chain)
- **Plate/serving ware:** White oval diner plates, standard diner silverware. Some items in baskets.

## 4. Website Typography System (PRIMARY for Agent 3)

| Element | Font Family | Weight | Size | Case | Color | Rendered Look |
|---------|------------|--------|------|------|-------|---------------|
| Headlines (H2) | Convergence | 700 | 22-36px | Sentence case | White (on dark) or #990000 (on light) | Clean, geometric sans-serif. Slightly condensed, modern but approachable. At display size, reads as sturdy and no-nonsense -- not decorative, not playful, just solid. |
| Body text | Open Sans | 500 | 20px | Sentence case | #666666 | Standard, highly readable sans-serif. Clean and neutral. |
| Nav links | Open Sans | 700 | 16px | UPPERCASE | #990000 | Bold uppercase dark red -- functional and direct |
| Menu item names | Open Sans | 700 | 20px | UPPERCASE | #666666 | Bold, simple, utilitarian |
| CTA buttons | Open Sans | 600 | 20px | Mixed | White on #990000 bg, or #990000 on #FBCE6D bg | Straightforward pill/rounded-rect buttons |
| Footer headings | Francois One | 600 | 24px | Sentence case | Dark | Slightly bolder, more characterful than Convergence |
| Logo | Custom hand-lettered | N/A | N/A | Title case | Warm orange-red | Vintage/rustic script with "Tee Jaye's" prominent, "Country Place Restaurants" as subtitle. Circular badge design with drop shadow. |

**Key for Agent 3:** Use Convergence for headlines and Open Sans for body text. The brand's typographic personality is **utilitarian and readable**, not decorative. The logo's hand-lettered warmth contrasts with the website's clean sans-serif system -- Agent 3 should lean into the warmth (Convergence + warm colors) rather than the corporate clean side.

## 8. Caption Architecture Guide

### Observed Patterns (from real posts)
- **Caption length:** Very short -- typically 1-3 sentences, often just one line
- **Hook style:** Direct and promotional -- announces the dish or promotion immediately
- **Body structure:** Simple description, sometimes with menu details and pricing
- **Emoji usage:** Minimal -- occasionally a food emoji but not consistently
- **Line break structure:** Usually single paragraph, no deliberate spacing
- **CTA style:** Simple direct calls: "Come on in, carry out or delivery!" or website URL
- **Hashtag placement:** End of caption, mixed into text
- **Hashtag count:** 3-6 per post (low)
- **Voice/personality:** Friendly, homey, no-frills grandma energy. Like a diner chalkboard sign.
- **Recurring phrases:** "Tee Jaye's," "Barnyard Buster," "Come on in," "carry out or delivery," "Don't forget to download our app"

## 12. Target Audience Persona

**Primary:** Families and working-class individuals in Central Ohio (Columbus, Newark, Zanesville) who value hearty, affordable, all-day breakfast and comfort food. 35-65 age range. Loyal regulars who know "their Tee Jaye's" by location.

**Secondary:** Seniors -- the brand offers dedicated Senior Breakfast and Senior Lunch/Dinner menus at reduced prices. Strong senior loyalty base.

**Tertiary:** College students and young adults near Columbus locations (Clintonville highlight suggests a younger-skewing location) looking for cheap, filling breakfast.

## 13. Words to Use / Words to Avoid

### Use
- "Down home," "homemade," "Granny's," "country," "comfort," "hearty"
- "Come on in," "y'all," "since 1970"
- "Barnyard Buster," "all-day breakfast"
- Location-specific references (Parsons Ave, Clintonville, Newark, Zanesville)
- "Carry out or delivery," "Order on DoorDash"
- Simple, warm, direct language

### Avoid
- Trendy food language: "artisanal," "craft," "elevated," "curated," "farm-to-table" (even if technically applicable)
- Instagram influencer speak: "obsessed," "I'm dead," "slay," "no cap"
- Pretentious descriptors: "deconstructed," "paired with," "drizzled reduction"
- Complex or long-winded captions -- keep it short and conversational
