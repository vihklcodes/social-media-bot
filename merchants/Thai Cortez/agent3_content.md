# Agent 3: Nano Banana Pro Content Architect — Thai Cortez (@thai.cortez)

**Date:** 2026-02-23 (updated design pass)
**Source:** agent1_brand.md (Brand Replication Blueprint) + agent2_occasions.md (Occasion Strategy Feb 24 – Mar 22, 2026)
**Format:** 12 occasions x (Nano Banana Pro image prompt + Instagram captions + hashtags + optional Story/Reel)

**Design philosophy:** Modern food editorial — elevated beyond the merchant's current amateur iPhone aesthetic to professional-grade promotional flyers. These should look like they were designed by a contemporary food branding agency. Bold typography, editorial food photography with dramatic lighting and depth of field, confident color blocking with brand colors, and clean modern compositions. The brand's deep red (#730101) and gold (#E7B103) are strong colors that deserve modern, bold treatment — not small-town traditional serif layouts on dark wood.

## Occasion 1: Thai Appetizer Showcase (Feb 24, Tuesday)

### Nano Banana Pro Image Prompt

**Format:** 1030 x 1350 px Instagram poster, portrait orientation.

**Layout pattern:** Full-Bleed Hero with Bold Overlay. The fried calamari fills the ENTIRE canvas edge-to-edge — no visible table, no plate borders, just golden crispy food dominating the full frame. The headline text sits directly over the food in the upper third, large and confident. This is a modern "food IS the poster" approach where the abundance of the close-up creates immediate impact. The CTA anchors the bottom 10%.

**Food photography:** Ultra-close-up of crispy golden fried calamari rings and tentacles — shot from directly above (overhead flat-lay) so the food fills every pixel of the canvas. Camera is CLOSE — individual batter textures, oil sheen, and crispy edges are visible. Dramatic directional side-lighting from the left creates defined shadows and bright highlights on the ridged batter surfaces, giving the calamari a sculptural, three-dimensional quality. Shallow depth of field — foreground calamari pieces are tack-sharp while pieces at the far edge softly blur. A small ramekin of amber-red sweet & sour sauce in the bottom-right corner provides a color accent. Scattered lime wedges and cilantro leaves are artfully placed between calamari pieces. Professional food photography quality — dramatic, appetizing, editorial.

**Environment:** The food IS the environment. White ceramic plate is barely visible as a subtle curve underneath — the calamari pile is so generous and close-up that it fills the frame. Only tiny glimpses of the plate surface show between pieces. Warm, dramatic directional lighting — golden highlights on the left side, soft shadows on the right.

**Color palette (Playwright-verified):**
- Food golden-brown tones: #CB9848 (warm curry golden), #BA8334 (deep golden-brown), #B1885B (food warm golden) — the dominant canvas color via the calamari batter
- Deep red-brown: #9E4709 on the dipping sauce
- Fresh green: #5E924E (cilantro leaf accents)
- Brand: #730101 (deep maroon — used as a horizontal text bar), white (#FFFFFF) for headline text

**Food styling:** Calamari shot from above fills the full frame — rings and tentacles create a textured, golden landscape. The close-up is so tight that individual batter crisp details are visible. Dipping sauce ramekin partially visible in one corner. Two lime wedges and scattered cilantro leaves provide green color pops against the golden field. This is food-as-texture, food-as-pattern — modern, maximal, scroll-stopping.

**Text design:**
- **Headline:** "CRISPY CALAMARI" in bold, condensed, modern sans-serif with geometric letterforms and heavy weight — clean, punchy, and contemporary (NOT thin traditional serif). ALL CAPS, tight letter-spacing. Color: white (#FFFFFF). Positioned in the upper-center (200-380px from top), centered horizontally. Font height approximately 80px, spanning about 50% of canvas width. A horizontal bar in #730101 (brand deep red) at 85% opacity, sized exactly to the headline with 20px padding above and below, sits behind the text — creating a clean, modern color block that ensures readability over the golden food.
- **Sub-headline CTA:** "Order now · link in bio!" in clean, geometric, light-weight sans-serif — modern and minimal. Color: #E7B103 (bright gold). Font height approximately 30px. Positioned at the bottom (1090-1160px from top), centered. No background bar — the gold text sits directly over the food where shadow areas provide natural contrast.
- **Text readability technique:** Brand color strip — a sleek horizontal bar in #730101 (brand deep red, 85% opacity) sits behind the headline. Sized precisely to the text width plus 40px total horizontal padding. This is a modern, intentional design element — not a gradient overlay, but a clean geometric color block that doubles as brand reinforcement. Crisp edges, no rounded corners.
- **Text placement:** Headline with color bar in the upper third. CTA at bottom. The middle 60% is unobstructed food.

**Eye flow:**
1. **FIRST:** The full-bleed golden calamari landscape stops the scroll with sheer visual texture and color saturation — the brain registers "crispy, golden, delicious" before reading anything.
2. **SECOND:** White "CRISPY CALAMARI" headline on the red brand bar punches through the food — the color contrast (red bar + white text over golden food) is impossible to miss.
3. **THIRD:** Gold CTA "Order now · link in bio!" at bottom provides the exit action.

**What NOT to include:** No borders, no frames, no stickers, no clip art, no full-width gradient overlays, no drop shadows, no thin traditional serif fonts, no visible table/surface.

## Occasion 2: National Chili Day — Thai Chili Reigns (Feb 26, Thursday)

### Nano Banana Pro Image Prompt

**Format:** 1030 x 1350 px Instagram poster, portrait orientation.

**Layout pattern:** Color Block Split — a bold vertical split divides the canvas. The left 38% is a solid fill of #730101 (brand deep red) with white and gold text stacked vertically. The right 62% is a dramatic close-up of the Seafood Green Curry. The hard geometric edge where the color block meets the food photo creates a clean, modern, magazine-editorial feel. This is confident, contemporary graphic design — not a photo with text overlaid.

**Food photography:** Dramatic close-up of Seafood Green Curry in a white ceramic bowl — shot at a low 25-degree angle (almost eye-level) for a dramatic, immersive perspective. The camera is close enough that the curry broth surface dominates, with visible pink shrimp, scallop pieces, mussels, and squid rising from the vibrant green coconut broth. Bright red Thai chili slices float on the glossy surface. Thai basil leaves are scattered on top. Dramatic directional lighting from the right creates a bright highlight on the curry's glossy surface and deep shadows on the left side of the bowl. The background is blurred (shallow depth of field) — just a warm, soft amber glow. Professional, moody, editorial food photography.

**Environment:** The food photograph occupies only the right 62% of the canvas. Background behind the bowl is softly blurred warm tones — no identifiable table or surface, just a warm bokeh glow. The left 38% is a solid brand color block. No visible table texture.

**Color palette (Playwright-verified):**
- Green curry tones: #715A16 (Thai curry green), #5E924E (fresh basil green)
- Curry golden surface: #CB9848 (coconut cream golden sheen)
- Deep red chili: #9E4709
- Brand deep red: #730101 (solid color block — left panel)
- Text: white (#FFFFFF) for headline, #E7B103 (gold) for CTA
- Bowl: clean white ceramic creating contrast

**Food styling:** Generous, abundant green curry filling the bowl — seafood pieces prominently visible above the broth line. The green color is vibrant and saturated. Red chili slices (3-4) provide striking contrast against the green. Thai basil leaves add depth. One lime wedge perched on the rim. Steam wisps optional for atmosphere. The presentation is elevated — professional food styling, not home cooking.

**Text design:**
- **Headline:** "THAI CHILI REIGNS" in bold, condensed, modern sans-serif with heavy weight and tight tracking. ALL CAPS. Color: white (#FFFFFF). Positioned on the solid #730101 red color block (left 38% of canvas), vertically centered (roughly 400-700px from top), left-aligned with 50px margin from left edge. Font height approximately 55px, stacked across three lines ("THAI" / "CHILI" / "REIGNS"), each word on its own line for dramatic vertical rhythm. Spans about 30% of canvas width.
- **Sub-headline CTA:** "Order now · link in bio!" in clean, geometric, light-weight sans-serif. Color: #E7B103 (bright gold). Font height approximately 26px. Positioned below the headline on the red panel, approximately 750px from top.
- **Text readability technique:** Full-bleed color block — the solid #730101 red panel provides 100% consistent contrast for white text. No transparency, no overlay on food — clean separation between text zone and food zone. This is the most reliable readability technique and the most modern.
- **Text placement:** All text lives on the left color block panel. Food is completely unobstructed on the right.

**Eye flow:**
1. **FIRST:** The bold red panel with stacked white "THAI CHILI REIGNS" grabs attention — the high-contrast text on the solid brand color is impossible to ignore.
2. **SECOND:** Eye moves right across the clean geometric edge to the vibrant green curry — the color contrast between the red panel and green food creates electric visual tension.
3. **THIRD:** Gold CTA "Order now · link in bio!" on the red panel catches the eye as it returns left.

**What NOT to include:** No borders, no frames, no stickers, no clip art, no gradient overlays, no drop shadows, no dark wood table visible. Clean geometric split only.

## Occasion 3: Winter Comfort Soup — Tom Kha (Mar 1, Sunday)

### Nano Banana Pro Image Prompt

**Format:** 1030 x 1350 px Instagram poster, portrait orientation.

**Layout pattern:** Minimal Editorial — generous breathing room and dramatic food hero. The top 30% is a warm, soft, out-of-focus gradient area (the steamy atmosphere above the bowl). The center 50% is the hero Tom Kha bowl, shot tight and dramatic. The bottom 20% has the CTA. Text overlays directly on the atmospheric top zone and the food. This is a modern "less is more" editorial approach — letting the food and atmosphere do the heavy lifting.

**Food photography:** Tom Kha soup shot from directly above (overhead flat-lay) so the bowl becomes a perfect circle of creamy white coconut broth. The overhead angle makes the soup surface a canvas — visible galangal slices, lemongrass stalks, kaffir lime leaves, chicken pieces, and mushroom slices create a beautiful pattern floating in the milky broth. A thin film of red chili oil creates amber-red rivulets across the white surface. Fresh cilantro leaves and a lime wedge at the bowl's edge. Dramatic warm lighting from one side creates a highlight crescent on the soup surface and the ceramic bowl edge. The bowl fills approximately 55% of the canvas width, centered. Background is a warm, softly blurred surface — not sharp dark wood, but a gentle warm amber-beige out-of-focus texture.

**Environment:** The background surface is softly out of focus — a warm, neutral tone (warm beige #D6B79B, blurred). This is NOT a sharp dark wood table — it's a modern, soft, atmospheric backdrop that keeps all attention on the soup bowl. Steam wisps rising from the soup create a gentle atmospheric haze in the upper portion of the frame. Warm, intimate lighting quality.

**Color palette (Playwright-verified):**
- Soup creamy tones: #D3C1A3 (cream), #D6B79B (warm beige), #CABCB3 (light warm cream)
- Golden coconut surface: #CB9848, #B1885B
- Red chili oil accent: #9E4709
- Fresh herb green: #5E924E (kaffir lime leaf, cilantro)
- Background: #D6B79B (warm beige, blurred soft)
- Brand: #730101 (headline — deep red on warm background), #E7B103 (gold CTA)

**Food styling:** Overhead view of Tom Kha — the circular bowl creates a satisfying geometric composition. Ingredients are artfully arranged in the broth: galangal coins in a loose cluster, lemongrass stalks angled across, mushroom slices fanned, chicken pieces visible. Red chili oil creates beautiful amber streaks across the white surface. Cilantro leaves float as green accents. Everything is visible through the milky broth — delicate, beautiful, almost artistic.

**Text design:**
- **Headline:** "WARM UP" in bold, wide, modern sans-serif with extra-heavy weight (900/Black) — chunky, warm, inviting letterforms. ALL CAPS, generous letter-spacing (3px). Color: #730101 (deep maroon red). Positioned in the top zone (150-300px from top) overlapping slightly with the steam/atmosphere area above the bowl, centered horizontally. Font height approximately 90px, spanning about 45% of canvas width. The large size and heavy weight ensure it reads clearly over the warm, soft background.
- **Dish name:** "Tom Kha" in clean, medium-weight sans-serif, sentence case. Color: #826401 (dark gold). Font height approximately 35px. Centered below the headline, approximately 330px from top.
- **Sub-headline CTA:** "Order now · link in bio!" in clean, light-weight sans-serif. Color: #E7B103 (bright gold). Font height approximately 28px. Positioned at bottom (1090-1160px from top), centered.
- **Text readability technique:** Bold text over atmospheric zone — the headline sits in the warm, soft, steamy area above/overlapping the soup bowl. The heavy font weight (Black/900) and deep maroon color read clearly over the warm beige blurred background (#D6B79B). No color block needed — the font weight does the work.
- **Text placement:** Headline at top in the atmospheric zone, CTA at bottom. Food is the undisputed center hero.

**Eye flow:**
1. **FIRST:** Bold, chunky "WARM UP" in deep maroon at top — the oversized, heavy letterforms feel warm and inviting, matching the soup's promise.
2. **SECOND:** Eye drops to the beautiful overhead Tom Kha bowl at center — the circular composition and creamy white surface with colorful ingredients draw the eye in.
3. **THIRD:** Gold CTA at bottom completes the reading path.

**What NOT to include:** No borders, no frames, no stickers, no clip art, no gradient overlays, no drop shadows, no sharp dark wood table, no thin serif fonts.

## Design Philosophy Notes

- **Elevated from amateur:** The merchant's existing feed is iPhone-quality casual food photos with no graphic design. These prompts describe professional-grade promotional flyers with editorial food photography, bold modern typography, and intentional graphic design elements (color blocks, geometric shapes, angled bars).
- **Modern sans-serif throughout:** Replaced the thin traditional PT Serif with bold, heavy, modern sans-serif typography across all 12 occasions. Heavy weights (700-900) ensure text reads over food. Condensed and wide proportions create visual variety.
- **Confident brand color usage:** The deep red (#730101) and gold (#E7B103) are used as bold graphic elements — solid color blocks, geometric shapes, text bars — not just text colors sitting on dark wood.
- **Professional photography direction:** Every prompt describes specific camera angles, lighting direction, depth of field, and styling that goes beyond "warm iPhone photo on dark table." Dramatic side-lighting, shallow depth of field, clean backgrounds, and editorial styling elevate every image.
- **Varied backgrounds:** Not every post is "food on dark wood table." Prompts use dark dramatic backgrounds (Occ 7), warm beige surfaces (Occ 6, 9), clean off-white (Occ 8, 12), solid color blocks (Occ 2, 5, 10), and atmospheric blur (Occ 3, 11).
- **Tone throughout captions:** Warm, authentic, educational — matching Mimi's voice. Captions are unchanged from the brand-matched original version.
- **CTA style:** "Order now — link in bio" throughout. No DoorDash mentions.
- **No gradient overlays, no drop shadows** — readability achieved through modern techniques: solid color blocks, heavy font weights, high-contrast pairings, and clean background zones.
