# Brand Intelligence: Los Ocampos

## 1. Brand Snapshot

Los Ocampos is a family-owned, multi-location authentic Mexican restaurant chain rooted in the Twin Cities (Saint Paul & Minneapolis, Minnesota). Founded in 2003 by Armando Ocampo and Lilia Zagal -- immigrants from Tejalpa, a small village in Morelos, Mexico -- the brand is now co-run by their son Julian Ocampo (University of St. Thomas 2018 graduate) and his wife Emma Ocampo (company controller). With 8+ locations spanning full-service restaurants & bars, taquerias, an express/skyway format, and the Machete Cocina Mexicana concept in Woodbury, the brand is built on Lilia's masa recipes and multi-generational family tradition. They position as "the Twin Cities' most authentic Mexican restaurant" -- heritage-driven, generous, affordable, and deeply embedded in the local community. Price range $31-50 per person, casual dining with full bar. Happy hour 3-6pm. Open late at select locations. DoorDash powerhouse with 960.8 MP weekly orders and 190.9 SF weekly orders. Instagram: @losocamporestaurants, ~2,856 followers, 701 posts. TikTok: @losocamporestaurants (same handle, same voice).

**Critical context:** In January 2025, all locations temporarily closed "due to ongoing federal activity" to protect employee and staff safety. A GoFundMe staff relief fund was created. Machete reopened for to-go orders January 20, 2025. The brand has since reopened. This closure is part of the brand's story and reinforces their deep community ties.

## 3. Text Overlay Specifications (Flyer / Promotional Style)

> **Direction: All posts should function as promotional flyers with text overlays that communicate the occasion and key message at a glance. The food is still the hero, but the text advertises what the post is about.**

- **Text on every image:** Yes -- every post is a flyer-style promotional image
- **Text density:** Headline (2-4 words) + optional sub-headline (1 short line). NOT walls of text.
- **Font style:** Bold sans-serif (e.g., Montserrat Bold, Oswald, or similar clean block typeface)
- **Font case:** ALL CAPS for headline text. Sentence case for sub-headline.
- **Font weight:** Bold to Extra Bold for headlines. Regular to Medium for sub-headlines.
- **Font size relative to image (1030 x 1350 canvas):**
  - Headline: ~30-40% of image width (~310-410px wide). Large enough to read at thumbnail size but not overwhelming.
  - Sub-headline: ~50-60% of image width, but thinner/lighter weight so it doesn't compete with headline.
- **Text alignment:** Centered horizontally
- **Text color:** White (primary). Subtle drop shadow (2px offset, 50% opacity black) for readability over food photos.
- **Brand accent color:** Dark green (#004612) -- can be used for small accent elements (a thin line, a small logo badge, or sub-headline color on lighter backgrounds)
- **Text placement:** Centered in the **bottom 12-18%** of the image. Sits above the food but doesn't overwhelm it. A subtle semi-transparent dark gradient (from transparent at top to ~30% black at bottom) behind the text area ensures readability.
- **Text decoration:** Minimal. No boxes, no frames, no stickers. Just clean text with drop shadow on a subtle gradient. Occasional thin horizontal line between headline and sub-headline.

**Text Content Guidelines:**
- Headline should name the occasion or key hook (e.g., "TACO TUESDAY," "NATIONAL MARGARITA DAY," "LENTEN FRIDAY")
- Sub-headline adds context or CTA (e.g., "The last feast before Lent," "Perfected since 2003," "Order on DoorDash")
- A viewer scrolling should understand what this flyer is advertising WITHOUT reading the caption

## 6. Caption Architecture Guide

**Based on 6 REAL verbatim captions from TikTok (@losocamporestaurants -- same account, same voice as Instagram). These are the closest verified proxy since Instagram captions could not be scraped directly.**

### Caption Patterns (from REAL posts):

- **Caption length:** Mix of **long-form storytelling (5-8 sentences, full paragraphs)** and occasional short promotional (1-2 sentences). The brand's default voice is LONG -- they tell stories, describe food in detail, share family legacy. This is NOT a "short punchy" brand.
- **Hook style (verbatim examples):**
  - Question: "Craving some delicious, authentic Mexican food?"
  - Imperative/emotional: "Show her you love her - take her out for tacos and margs at Los Ocampo!"
  - Exclamation: "Hot and ready for you!"
  - Save prompt: "Save this post for your new favorite Taco Tuesday spot in St. Paul"
  - Welcome: "Welcome to Los Ocampo!"
- **Body structure:** Tell origin stories, describe food with sensory detail (lists every ingredient), share the family legacy narrative, call out multiple locations. Typical flow: Hook ‚Üí food description with specific ingredients ‚Üí family/legacy tie-in ‚Üí CTA
- **Emoji usage:** Food emojis (üåÆü•ë), celebration emojis (üéâ‚ú®), drink emojis (üçπüçª). Placed at sentence openings AND endings. Moderate usage -- 4-8 per post. Used as punctuation, not decoration.
- **Line break structure:** Dense paragraphs -- NOT one-line-per-thought. Flowing storytelling with sentences running together.
- **CTA style (verbatim examples):**
  - "Gather your familia and join us for a feast at Los Ocampo today!"
  - "Tag your date and treat her to the best tacos in town!"
  - "Save this post for your new favorite Taco Tuesday spot"
  - "Gather your amigos and familia this weekend, and we'll see you soon!"
  - "Bring your friends, grab a margarita (or 3), and let's taco 'bout a good time!"
- **Hashtag placement:** End of caption, after the CTA
- **Hashtag count range:** 6-7 (compact set, not 20+)
- **Core hashtag set:** #losocampo #mexicanfood #stpaulfoodie #twincitiesfoodie #eatertwincities #mneats (occasionally swaps #mneats for specific tags like #tacos #tacotuesdays)
- **Bilingual elements:** Natural Spanish woven in: "familia," "amigos," dish names (Molcajete Mar y Tierra, salsa borracha, arrachera, Molcajete de Carnes, Molcajete de Mariscos). Never forced, never full-Spanish captions.
- **Voice/personality:** Proud family storyteller. Warm, enthusiastic, community-oriented. Writes like someone who genuinely loves their food and family legacy. Uses food puns occasionally ("let's taco 'bout a good time!").
- **Recurring phrases:** "recipes passed down through generations," "since 2003," "authentic Mexican," "gather your familia," "the Twin Cities," "Los Ocampo near you"
- **Food descriptions:** Extremely specific and visceral -- lists every ingredient with sensory adjectives: "juicy arrachera steak, savory al pastor, tender chicken, flavorful chorizo, and mixed seafood, all simmering in our salsa borracha"

## 8. Tone & Voice Encoding

**Brand tone in 3 words:** Proud, Warm, Generous

**Personality archetype:** The proud family matriarch who feeds everyone -- confident in her food, warm to everyone who walks through the door, tells you the whole story of how this dish came to be. Zero pretension, maximum heart. Writes long because there's a lot to be proud of.

**Words/phrases frequently used (from real captions):**
- "familia" / "amigos"
- "authentic Mexican" / "authentic Mexican flavors"
- "since 2003" / "recipes passed down through generations"
- "family legacy" / "family recipes"
- "gather your familia and join us"
- "the Twin Cities" / "St. Paul" / "Los Ocampo near you"
- Sensory food words: "juicy," "savory," "tender," "flavorful," "sizzling," "piping hot," "mouthwatering," "irresistible"

**Words/phrases never used:**
- Artisanal, curated, elevated, craft
- Foodie, hidden gem, underrated, slept-on
- Vibe, aesthetic, mood, energy
- Leverage, utilize, innovative, synergy
- "Mexican-inspired" (they ARE Mexican)
- Fusion, twist, reimagined, deconstructed
- Game changer, next level, slaps different
- Any language that sounds like a trendy startup or corporate chain

**Energy level:** 7/10 -- enthusiastic and warm but not screaming. More "come eat, you know it's good, let us tell you the story" than "OMG YOU NEED THIS!!!"

## 10. Target Audience Persona

**Primary: "The Twin Cities Mexican Food Family"**
- Latino and non-Latino families in Saint Paul / Minneapolis
- Age 25-55
- Value authenticity over trendiness
- Regulars who order the same thing every time
- Friday/Saturday night dinner crowd, Sunday family meals
- DoorDash power users who reorder weekly
- Price-conscious but willing to pay for quality and portions
- Diverse crowd (per reviews and press) -- the brand crosses cultural lines through universal love of good food

**Secondary: "The Weeknight Delivery Orderer"**
- Age 22-40
- Orders delivery 2-3x/week via DoorDash
- Discovers through DoorDash browse, not Instagram
- Loves quesabirria tacos, burritos, and horchata
- Could become an in-person customer if social media creates connection
- Late-night crowd at taqueria locations

**Tertiary: "The Catering Client"**
- Event planners, office managers, family gathering organizers
- Needs reliable, crowd-pleasing Mexican food at scale
- Values large portions, consistent quality, easy ordering
- Graduation parties, weddings, corporate events, holiday gatherings
